"Self-Portrait" Project

Made for ARS 324 at Stony Brook University
Applications coded in Java, JavaFX using NetBeans IDE
Drawings done in Microsoft Paint, GIMP 2
Live video recorded using iPhone SE
Screen recordings done in OBS Studio
Music used:
"Harmony of Heroes - King DedeDnB (Kirby - King Dedede Theme Remix)" by Brutal MMK
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZ2YztsTGdI

This was a fun project to do! At the end of ARS 324 (Graphic Design), we had to make a video that communicated our personalities (a description deliberately left vague). I wanted to convey my experiences with both programming and making digital media. More importantly, I wanted to show how I could use both skill sets for the same project.

Fun Fact: The talking sprite featured at the end of the video had two forms - mouth open and mouth closed. These toggled with every keystroke. If I pressed keys an odd number of times, the program would leave the sprite's mouth hanging open (and his face looking kind of stupid).


Editing a Profile Picture

Drawn using GIMP 2
Recorded in OBS Studio
Sped up in Adobe After Effects
Music used:
"Style Funk" by Kevin MacLeod (incompetech.com)
Licensed under Creative Commons: By Attribution 3.0
https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/3.0/

The original recording of this video was around five hours long. Speeding it up shortened it to just five minutes, but presented its own problems - zooming in and out is disorienting at this speed. The proportions also don't look quite right, as the head seems too wide compared to the shoulders and hips. Were I to do this again, I would pay more attention to both problems - assuming, of course, I could be pulled away from the convenience of Adobe Illustrator.
