SBU Running Club Wiki

Made for CCS 395 at Stony Brook University
Created using TiddlyWiki
Open in new window

CCS 395 was an odd class in that it was dedicated to wikis - general-purpose collections of information like Wikipedia or smaller sites for specific topics. The curriculum went the processes by which they collect and spread information, and how just having that information does not grant knowledge and understanding.
Looking back at my final project for the class, I can definitely say that this is true. I made this wiki as a guide for the EBoard members that would take over the Running Club. While some of the information here might have been useful as a reference, no wiki alone was going to fully prepare them for club leadership. Only getting stuck in responsibilities and deadlines was going to do that. Thankfully for everyone involved, they've done a fine job thus far.


Pitch Presentation - Stony Brook Video Games

Made for CSE 300 at Stony Brook University
Created in Google Slides
Open in new window

The Computer Science major at Stony Brook requires all students to take CSE 300 - Technical Communications. I really liked this class, because it was a refreshing break from theory and programming. Instead, it focused on "soft skills" like building a resumé, writing a cover letter, and presenting an idea.
Admittedly, I should have fleshed out the idea I was presenting more. I knew I wanted to make a video game that used Stony Brook as a setting, but I didn't know much else. Thankfully, this pitch was less about making an actual game than it was about convincing other people that it was possible. Coincidentally, I decided to make a game for my senior project not too long afterwards.
